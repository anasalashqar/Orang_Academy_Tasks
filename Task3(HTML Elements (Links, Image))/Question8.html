is an interdisciplinary field that combines principles from both electrical engineering and computer science. It focuses on the design, development, and application of computer systems, software, and hardware. The foundation of computer engineering lies in understanding how computers work from the ground up, including both the physical components like processors, memory, and circuit boards, and the software that allows the hardware to function. A computer engineer is tasked with designing microprocessors, embedded systems, networks, and even entire computing infrastructures that power everything from smartphones to supercomputers. The field is expansive, touching on areas like artificial intelligence, robotics, cybersecurity, machine learning, data science, and internet of things (IoT). With advancements in technology and the rapid pace of innovation, computer engineering plays.

critical role in shaping modern society, driving the development of cutting-edge technologies and systems that impact nearly every industry, from healthcare to entertainment, automotive to telecommunications. At the heart of computer engineering is problem-solving, as engineers must navigate the complexities of both hardware and software, ensuring they work seamlessly together. In today's world, the integration of computer systems into every facet of life makes computer engineering not just an exciting and challenging field, but also one of immense importance and relevance in solving real-world problems.
